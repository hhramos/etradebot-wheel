🟢 PHASE A — Pre-Entry (Before Opening Any Wheel)

✅ Capital Check

  • Do I have enough collateral (cash or BP) for this position?
  • Will this position keep cash buffer ≥ $300?
  • Is concentration ≤ 5% of account in any single ticker?
  • Total deployed capital ≤ 80% of NAV?
Why: Wheel strategy survives drawdowns only with reserves. Over-deployment forces forced liquidation at the worst time. The 5% ticker rule prevents single-stock blowups from killing the account.

✅ Ticker Eligibility

  • Stock price ≤ "comfortable trade" threshold (5% of account)?
  • Liquidity check: Open Interest ≥ 500 at chosen strike
  • Bid-ask spread ≤ 10% of mid price?
  • Stock has options market (not all stocks do)
  • Beta acceptable (not extreme like meme stocks)
  • I'd be comfortable owning 100 shares if assigned?
Why: Wide spreads eat your premium. Thin OI means you can't get out cleanly. The "comfortable owning" test catches the single biggest beginner mistake — selling puts on stocks you'd never actually buy.

✅ Strike Selection

  • Strike chosen for delta target (-0.20 to -0.30 for safer, ATM for higher yield)
  • Strike provides OTM cushion ≥ 5% (preferably 8-12%)
  • Capital efficiency: premium ÷ collateral ≥ 3% per cycle (≥36% annualized)
Why: Delta tells you probability of being assigned. -0.30 = 30% chance. -0.50 = 50% (ATM). Pick based on your risk tolerance. The 3% per cycle minimum filters out trades not worth the capital lockup.

✅ Expiry Selection

  • Target 35-45 DTE for theta sweet spot
  • Use standard MONTHLY expirations (3rd Friday of month)
  • Avoid weekly options unless explicitly desired
  • Expiry does NOT include any earnings date for the underlying
Why: 35-45 DTE captures the cleanest theta decay. Monthlies have the best liquidity. Weekly options have wider spreads and pin-risk. Earnings inside expiry = IV crush + gap risk that destroys premium math.

✅ Calendar/Event Risk

  • No earnings report within 14 days of expiry
  • No ex-dividend date in window (for short calls)
  • No FOMC meeting on or near expiry week
  • No known major event (CPI release, election) within 5 days of expiry
Why: Earnings can move stocks ±15% overnight, instantly putting puts ITM. Ex-div on short calls causes early assignment risk. Macro events can spike IV (good for entries, bad for held positions).

✅ IV Regime Check

  • Check current IV at chosen strike
  • HIGH IV (>25%) → use 50% profit target, no DTE rule
  • BORDERLINE (18-25%) → use 50% target + 14-21 DTE backstop
  • LOW IV (<18%) → use 50% target only (no DTE), let theta finish
Why: Different IV regimes need different exit rules. High IV = fast theta, 50% target hits quickly. Low IV = slow theta, DTE-based exits abandon too much premium. See memory/feedback_exit_rules.md for the full framework.

✅ Order Construction

  • Limit order (NEVER market for options)
  • Limit price = mid or slightly above (give 1-2 cents over mid)
  • Duration: GTC (so it works overnight if not immediately filled)
  • Single contract first; scale only after confirming fill behavior
Why: Options spreads are wide enough that market orders can fill 10-20% worse than mid. GTC orders catch overnight liquidity. Starting with 1 contract validates your fill price before scaling.

🟡 PHASE B — Mid-Cycle Monitoring

✅ Daily Check (5 min)

  • Has price moved significantly? (±5%+)
  • Did the underlying drop into ITM territory?
  • Any breaking news on the underlying?
  • Any change in option's bid-ask spread (liquidity check)?

✅ Weekly Check (15 min)

  • Did BTC profit target hit?
  • Re-check earnings calendar (sometimes announced/changed)
  • Re-check ex-dividend dates
  • Track DTE vs the 21 DTE threshold

🚨 Red Flags (act immediately if hit)

  • Stock breaks key technical support
  • Delta crossed -0.50 (now ITM territory)
  • Position P/L worse than -50% of credit received
  • Major news/earnings surprise
  • Liquidity dries up (OI dropping, spread widening)
Why: Wheel positions are short premium — they have positive theta but negative gamma. The longer a position stays profitable, the safer it is. Red flags indicate gamma is winning. Acting early is better than panicking late.

🔴 PHASE C — Exit Decisions

Path 1: BTC at 50% Target (Primary)

  • Did automated GTC BTC order fill?
  • Did I book the realized profit in memory?
  • Is capital now available for next deployment?
  • Place new wheel entry within 1-2 days

Path 2: Approaching 21 DTE

  • Has 50% target NOT filled by 21 DTE?
  • Is position still in HIGH IV regime?
    • If yes: 50% target still valid; consider holding 7 more days
    • If shifted to BORDERLINE/LOW: consider 21 DTE exit
  • Calculate "premium remaining" vs "premium captured so far"
  • If captured ≥40%: consider closing for safety
  • If captured <30%: more value in letting it work to expiry

Path 3: Defensive Close (Roll/Loss)

  • Is position worse than -50% of credit?
  • Has underlying thesis changed?
  • Is there a better opportunity for the capital?
  • Calculate cost to close vs continue holding
Why: The 50% target is mathematically optimal for high-IV environments because the remaining 50% of premium decays much slower than the first 50%. Cutting at 21 DTE protects against gamma but only matters when premium remaining is small.

🔄 PHASE D — Roll Decision Tree

Same Stock, Next Cycle (Most Common)

  • Pick next standard monthly expiry (next 3rd Friday)
  • Re-evaluate strike based on current price:
    • Stock UP → roll up strike (capture richer premium)
    • Stock UNCHANGED → same strike
    • Stock DOWN → roll down strike (defensive)
  • Re-verify earnings clear of new expiry
  • Re-verify IV regime
  • Set new GTC BTC at 50% target

Switch Stock

  • Has fundamental thesis on original stock changed?
  • Better risk-reward elsewhere (from latest screener)?
  • Diversification considerations met?

Skip a Cycle

  • All candidates have earnings risk → wait
  • Market in unusual state (FOMC week, election, etc.) → wait
  • Cash needed for assignment management
Why: Rolling forward with a re-evaluated strike isn't just mechanics — it incorporates new market intelligence. Same-strike on autopilot ignores stock movement. Sometimes the right move is to skip the cycle entirely.

🟣 PHASE E — Assignment Management (If Put Goes ITM)

Day After Assignment

  • Confirm 100 shares received in account
  • Calculate effective cost basis: strike - premium received
  • Update memory/project_state.md with new position
  • Decide: hold for recovery vs sell CC immediately?

If Selling Covered Call (Cycle 2 of Wheel)

  • Choose strike at or above cost basis (so call-away = profit)
  • 30-45 DTE expiry
  • Premium ≥ 1% of stock price per cycle
  • Place GTC BTC at 50% profit target

If Holding Shares Bare (No CC)

  • Only if expecting near-term rebound
  • Set price alert at cost basis level
  • Re-evaluate daily/weekly
  • Have exit plan (when do I cut losses?)

If Called Away (CC Cycle Closed)

  • Calculate cycle profit (premium + share gain)
  • Update memory
  • Decide: re-buy same stock for new wheel OR redeploy capital elsewhere?
Why: Assignment isn't failure — it's the SECOND half of the wheel. The whole strategy assumes you'd be happy to own these stocks at the strike price. The CC cycle that follows often makes more money than the original put cycle.

💰 PHASE F — Capital Discipline (Account-Level)

Position Sizing Rules

  • Max 10 concurrent wheels (operational complexity)
  • Max 8% of NAV in any single position
  • Max 80% total deployed (always keep 20% buffer)
  • Keep $300+ minimum cash for emergency

Diversification Targets

  • No more than 30% in any sector
  • Mix of stock prices ($5-$35 ideal range)
  • Mix of betas (some defensive + some aggressive)
  • Avoid correlated tickers (don't have CLSK + MARA + RIOT all simultaneously)

Income Targets

  • Monthly premium goal: ≥ 1.5% of account value
  • Annualized goal: 18-25% net premium income
  • Reinvestment: roll all premiums into new wheels (compounding)
Why: Wheel strategies fail when account is overexposed. Concentration risk means one bad stock can wipe out months of profits. The 80% deployment cap ensures you can survive a drawdown without forced liquidations.

⛔ PHASE G — Common Mistakes to Avoid

Discipline Checks

  • NEVER use market orders for options
  • NEVER sell naked calls (unlimited risk)
  • NEVER trade through major earnings without understanding the risk
  • NEVER chase trades after a missed entry
  • NEVER scale up a losing position to "average down"
  • NEVER skip the GTC BTC order at entry
  • NEVER hold to expiry hoping for the last 5%
  • NEVER ignore the IV regime when setting exit rules
  • NEVER override your own rules for "this one special case"

Documentation Discipline

  • Update memory/project_state.md after every fill
  • Update memory/open_tasks.md when status changes
  • Save trade records (order numbers, prices, dates)
  • Run weekly screener (Wednesdays)
  • Backup full project monthly
Why: These mistakes don't just hurt occasionally — they're how accounts get wiped out. Discipline is what separates profitable wheelers from people who blow up. Documentation lets you analyze WHY trades worked or failed.
